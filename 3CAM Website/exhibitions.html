Celebrate AAPI Heritage Month at 3CAM


During the month of May, 3CAM will showcase various exhibitions and projects celebrating Asian Americans, Pacific Islanders, and their contributions to the enrichment of American society as a whole.


In October of 1978, President Jimmy Carter first proclaimed what was Pacific/Asian-American Heritage Week with the passing of House Joint Resolution 1007. It consisted of the first ten days in May. President George H.W. Bush subsequently signed a bill passed by Congress in 1990 that extended Asian-American Heritage Week into a month-long event, officially being designated Asian-Pacific Heritage Month in 1992.

More Exhibits and Events

The Art of the Lawless

3CAM takes an in-depth look at the culture and lives of those living in "Slab City" - Imperial County's off-the-grid and unincorporated community where outcasts, squatters, and free spirits call home. Experience the various forms of art, poetry and spoken word performances of Slab City's residents.

World of Orchids

The annual orchid showcase is located inside 3CAM's Mackenzie Bezos Botanical Conservatory. Don't miss your chance to experience the beauty and grace of these orchids.

LEGEND - Classical Composers Living the 'Thug Life'

French branding and packaging design studio Blackthorn Design serves up some beer (or at least some very compelling design mock-ups) featuring portraits of classical composers such as Mozart, Beethoven, and Chopin paired with visual cues of American street culture.
